📜 Etymology

The word "computer" comes from the Latin word "computare" — meaning to calculate. A computer is a programmable, electronic data processing machine that is becoming an indispensable part of our daily life.

💡 Key Idea: Computers don't just calculate — they process data automatically, accurately, and at incredible speeds far beyond human ability.
🗑️ G·I·G·O

Garbage In, Garbage Out — If you give a computer wrong input, it will produce wrong output. A computer is only as accurate as the data you give it!

⚡ The 5 Superpowers of Computers

⚡

Speed

Performs millions or billions of calculations per second — far beyond human capability!

🎯

Accuracy

Operates at 100% accuracy. Errors only happen due to GIGO — wrong input = wrong output.

🤖

Automation

Functions automatically once essential data and instructions are provided by the user.

🔋

Diligence

Never gets bored or tired. It can perform repetitive tasks for days with the exact same accuracy.

🎨

Versatility

Can do many different things — from simple math to playing games to complex logical tasks!

Section 2 — Capabilities vs. Limitations

0 / 14

Despite its superpowers, a computer is still a "dull machine". It relies entirely on human intelligence and electricity to function. Let's explore what computers CAN and CANNOT do!

💪 What Computers CAN Do

  • ✔️ Perform tasks automatically with 100% accuracy
  • ✔️ Execute repetitive tasks at the exact same speed constantly
  • ✔️ Store massive amounts of data and retrieve it instantly
  • ✔️ Perform different tasks in vastly different areas simultaneously

❌ What Computers CANNOT Do

  • ✖️ Think, learn, reason, or react like a human being
  • ✖️ Make right decisions independently
  • ✖️ Operate without electricity or a battery power source
  • ✖️ Produce accurate output if given inaccurate input data

Section 3 — The Modern Impact

0 / 14

The widespread use of computers brings immense advantages to society, but it also introduces new disadvantages that we must carefully manage. Let's explore both sides!

✅ Advantages

➤ Unmatched Speed: Faster than any other device ever invented by humans.
➤ Global Connectivity: Enables vast communication networks like the Internet.
➤ Permanent Memory: Provides large and permanent storage facilities for programs and data.

❌ Disadvantages

➤ Cyber Crime: Opens doors to hacking, cracking, and malicious software (viruses, worms, Trojans).
➤ High Costs: Still an expensive device requiring frequent repair and maintenance.
➤ Data Vulnerability: Device or program failures can lead to massive loss of critical information.
